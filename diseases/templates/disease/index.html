About Parkinson's disease

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement. It is caused by the progressive loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain, particularly in an area called the substantia nigra. Dopamine is a chemical messenger in the brain that helps to regulate movement, mood, and other functions.

As dopamine levels decrease, people with Parkinson's disease may experience tremors, stiffness, slow movement, and difficulty with balance and coordination.
